The Ready-to-Copy Prompt

When you don't want to install anything, paste this prompt, fill the brackets, and get on-brand captions in one shot. It's the skill's logic, compressed into a single message.

You are writing social content in my brand's voice. Use this brief and follow it exactly.

Brand: [BRAND NAME]
Audience: [WHO READS THIS, AND WHAT THEY WANT—one sentence]
Voice in three words: [e.g. confident, calm, strategic]
Three do's: [do 1] / [do 2] / [do 3]
Three don'ts: [don't 1] / [don't 2] / [don't 3]
Approved proof points: [real stats or results only—leave blank if none]

Today's post is about: [TOPIC]
The goal of this post is to: [save / share / reply / click—pick one]

Write:
1. Three caption options—short, medium and long.
2. One scroll-stopping hook for the first line.

Rules: match the voice words in every line, honor all three do's and don'ts, use
only the approved proof points (never invent a stat, result or quote), Title Case
headlines, sentence-case body, no emoji, closed em-dashes (word—word). End with one
line reminding me to check each draft against: "Would we actually say this?"
Part three

The Guide: Briefing AI Without Losing Your Voice

Read this once. It's the thinking behind the skill—why generic AI content happens, and the three inputs that fix it.

Why AI Content Sounds Generic

AI writes to the average of everything it has read. Without a brief, it defaults to the safest, most common phrasing—which is exactly the voice every other brand is also getting. The model isn't the problem. The missing brief is.

The Three Inputs That Fix It

On-brand AI content comes from giving the model three things every time—voice, audience and intent.

  • Voice. Name your tone in plain words, then give three do's and three don'ts. "Confident, calm, strategic. Do use short declaratives. Don't use hype or emoji."
  • Audience. One sentence on who's reading and what they're trying to do. The model writes differently for a founder than for a first-time buyer.
  • Intent. What this specific post is for—save, share, reply or click. A caption written to start replies looks nothing like one written to drive a click.

The Workflow

Brief once, generate in batches, edit for truth. Use the skill or the prompt to set voice, audience and intent, ask for a week of posts in one go, then read each draft against one question—would we actually say this? Cut anything you wouldn't. The kit gets you to a strong first draft; your judgment makes it true.

One Rule to Keep It Honest
